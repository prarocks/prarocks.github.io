Drought Analysis and Agricultural Water Needs

Studying Climate Impacts on Irrigation in Mahakali, Nepal

Overview

This project examines how climate-driven droughts affect irrigation and crop production in the Mahakali Irrigation Command Area, Nepal. Using drought indices and crop modeling, it assesses water needs for maize, paddy, and wheat. This work is part of a manuscript in preparation/publication. Contact me at prasanna1dahal@gmail.com for details.

Key Findings

  • Analyzed drought patterns from 1985–2024 using Standardized Precipitation Index (SPI) and Standardized Precipitation Evapotranspiration Index (SPEI).
  • Simulated Net Irrigation Water Requirement (NIWR) with FAO’s AquaCrop model, correlating drought indices with irrigation demand.
  • Identified a strong relationship between meteorological droughts and increased NIWR, impacting the Factor of Safety of irrigation systems.

Future Work

  • Expand dataset to include 2030–2050 climate projections for long-term drought resilience planning.
  • Integrate remote sensing data to improve drought monitoring accuracy.
  • Develop adaptive irrigation strategies based on NIWR-drought correlations.
